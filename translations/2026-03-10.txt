Alone in Kesennuma.  
By Shigesato Itoi

I’ve arrived here.  
Right away, I’m meeting people I haven’t seen in ages, and it’s a joy.  
When I saw the elderly lady, we both instantly said, "You haven’t changed a bit. You look well."  
I think she’s about ten years my senior.  
After saying I looked well, she added, "And you seem interesting."  
Never before have I received such delightful words.  
From now on, I’ll live in a way that makes people say I seem interesting.

I also got to meet someone else older than me, a visitor from Hokkaido—Mr. Oda from R-Tei. That made me happy too.  
It was exactly like that saying, "A friend comes from afar."  
I’ve been influenced by many people, but what this mentor has done and shared with me seems to shape my current (especially recent) way of thinking.  
When it gets a bit warmer, I’m definitely going to visit again.

There’s a way of thinking that I find completely true.  
For example, "More haste, less speed," which I’ve come to understand deeply.  
But I also completely agree with "Make hay while the sun shines."  
When faced with the choice of rushing or taking the long way, you have to "decide" each time.  
While talking about various things, I realized this happens a lot.  
Sometimes, it really is "more haste, less speed."  
Don’t rush, don’t be in such a hurry.  
Surely, you’ve failed before by taking shortcuts, haven’t you?  
But then again, while you’re being calm, countless opportunities slip by.  
So much time is wasted dithering.  
I often tell myself and others, "Do it immediately; make hay while the sun shines!"  
Everyone has experienced both sides and can relate to those feelings.

Many things are truly "both," but we "decide" based on the situation or circumstances.  
In other words, life is about "decisions."  
Here in a Kesennuma hotel, I’m jotting down these thoughts before I sleep.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today as well.  
Ending my writing here is also a "decision" I just made.
