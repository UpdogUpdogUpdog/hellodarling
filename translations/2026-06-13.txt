I jotted down a note outside the cinema.  
Shigesato Itoi

・You know how a single drop of rain can keep falling on a stone until, before you know it, it wears a hole right through? Thoughts can be like that too. Even the tiniest drop of an idea, if pondered often enough, might carve out a "word"—a groove in the stone of your mind.

Yesterday, I watched a Spanish film called "Siráat." I won't give away much of the plot, but it was extraordinary—the images washed over me in waves. The script, the music (sound design), the direction, the actors, the visuals, the set design... everything felt pitch-perfect. It's the kind of masterpiece you can't find a single flaw in. You might call it a grand comedy of errors, a massive tragicomedy, or intensely philosophical. Its realism could almost make you forget it's not a documentary. From the initial spark of "I want to make a movie like this" to the triumphant "It's finished!"—I was overwhelmed by the mental, physical, financial, and temporal endurance required to bring it to life.

Watching this incredible film must have collided with my longstanding, rain-like thoughts. The word "LIFE" stood tall and proud again. I don't want to just talk about this as an idea; I want to write it down as a word, like a short poem, placed in front of me.

◆ Don't skip the details.  
   That's like skipping parts of life itself.

◆ A single day mirrors the shape of an entire life.  
   What you seek today is the same as what you'll seek your whole life.

◆ Cheap, fast, in bulk?  
   If you want a life that's cheap, fast, and in bulk, be my guest.

I scribbled these notes after leaving the theater. Shibuya's station area was bustling with countless LIFEs. Everyone, please go on living as you wish.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" today.  
LIFE is life, living, existence, rawness, and journey. It's important.
