Alright, here we go—year 29 has officially begun.
Shigesato Itoi

・As of today, "Hobonichi" has entered its 29th year.
  Since yesterday, June 6, was the anniversary,
  today marks the start of year 29.
  I wonder if everyone who's busy keeping things running realizes it—year 29 is here!

  But really, when people ask what kind of company "Hobonichi" is,
  I suspect even we aren't entirely sure.
  Isn't that a problem, you might ask?
  Typically, you know what you're working towards,
  so you can strive in that direction, right?
  When there's a clear goal, you can work backwards to figure out what needs doing now...
  But we haven't been nagged about that too much.
  Maybe because people thought there wasn't much point in saying it.

  Even without being told, we're managing just fine.

  Seeds are sown in our "thoughtful open spaces," different from everyone else's,
  and those seeds grow—or don't—through sun, clouds, and rain.
  Leaves do their photosynthesis thing, flowers bloom,
  roots drink up water and nutrients from the soil,
  and if all goes well, fruit appears.
  If you get too caught up in chasing the fruit as the goal, you miss out.
  Instead, wondering what might grow, appreciating beauty, finding things amusing—
  it's this journey that nurtures us while we nurture.

  Having been nurtured, we're full of energy to find more "thoughtful open spaces" and plant more seeds.
  Maybe fields or forests will emerge.
  People, as well as the spaces themselves, grow.
  Bees buzz, birds and animals come by.
  Follow a rabbit and you might find a curious hole.
  It's fascinating, and because it's fascinating, we can play with all our might.
  Exhausted yet full of gratitude for our companions.
  Now, we've reached the final day of the "Living Joy Exhibition."

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" today.
AI is amazing, but I want to believe humans are even more so.
