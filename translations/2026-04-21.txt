What is AI missing?
Shigesato Itoi

・It's often said that the main difference between AI and humans is that AI lacks a "body" and a "heart." I agree, but I've been pondering if there's a better way to express this. Call it a bit of intellectual nitpicking, but here's another thought: Someday, we might be able to create something akin to a "body." And perhaps, something resembling a "heart" might eventually emerge.

In my amateurish way, I've been thinking about this for some time now.

AI doesn't have what I'd call a "life."

Even the most advanced AI systems come equipped with vast networks of mind-boggling amounts of information, capable of performing calculations and storing data. We already know they're getting smarter by the day. If we combine them with robotics, it's conceivable that they could engage in physical activities too. But they only perform tasks when needed; they don't eat different foods, engage in idle chatter, take leisurely walks, get casually asked something by someone, savor the taste of food (or not), experience unrequited love, feel liked by others, get up to use the bathroom, or sit through a boring TV show. They don't partake in the ambiguous "life" we experience, nor do they encounter the random coincidences life throws our way, and they don't change through such serendipity.

In contrast, humans swim in the sea of chance that is "life."

"Life," in English, is "LIFE." And "LIFE" is "existence." Furthermore, "LIFE" is "vitality."

When I think about it this way, you could say AI lacks "LIFE." Yes, indeed, humans have "LIFE," and there's something truly joyful about that.

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" again today. Life can be fleeting and difficult, but it's undeniably fascinating.
