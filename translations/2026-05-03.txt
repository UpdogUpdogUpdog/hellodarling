The Day I Decided to Enjoy My Work  
by Shigesato Itoi

・At the "Kayo Music Café" held at Shibuya Parco's "Almost Sunday," I had booked a seat yesterday because the guest was Mino from "Mino Music." But, you know, there was another event I had been eagerly looking forward to on this May 2nd for quite some time. It was the world title match between Naoya Inoue and Junto Nakatani. Despite the clash in schedule, I decided to prioritize our own "field" and headed to Shibuya. Naturally, it was a lot of fun, but even if I hurried home, it would be around 9 p.m. I thought the match would be over by then, but lo and behold, it hadn't started yet. Seriously? Did I actually make it in time? Thanks to that, I got to watch both Mino and the Inoue-Nakatani fight in full, respectfully skipping the honorifics.

The match was incredible—this is what it means to be breathless. Whenever I watch boxing matches on TV, I keep glancing at the "3-minute countdown," but with the bout between Inoue and Nakatani, I couldn't take my eyes off for a moment. This might have been a first for me. I imagine the impression of someone like me saying "It was amazing" is on a completely different level from someone who truly understands boxing, but from either viewpoint, saying "It was amazing" is the mark of an ideal match. It might just go down as a "legendary match."

・It's impressive when "those who know recognize greatness," but there are times when "even ordinary people can see it too." When there’s a depth that only "insiders" can appreciate and an appeal that captivates "ordinary people," it's quite something. There really are things that encompass both. Once, at a company, when the elevator doors opened, I caught sight of a monochrome "sketch" hanging on the hall wall. It felt "somehow amazing," so I got back on the elevator to take another look. Finding out it was a "Picasso" made me nod in admiration. The calligraphy of "Ryokan" printed in magazines or the poems of "Michio Mado" are likely examples of the same phenomenon.

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" again today. Every time I encounter something great, I resolve to enjoy my own work.
