Even Grannies Were Once Young

・We often think that grandpas and grandmas have always been old, but they too were once mischievous youths or bubbly young girls. Back then, they did things and had thoughts that would be unthinkable now—a bit wild and fueled by youthful recklessness. I find myself pondering this, setting aside my own experiences for a moment.

If you look closely, those grandpas and grandmas once had their own innocent and pure-hearted thoughts. They may not show it now, but the fact is, they weren’t born old. If you really think about it, it's plainly true, isn't it?

This also applies to those adults in their thirties and forties, busy with work and acting all mature. They too were once fresh-faced newcomers. It's like a second grader looking at a first grader and saying, "Aren’t they cute? The first graders have arrived," trying to act like a mentor. It's fascinating, really, how people keep doing this throughout life.

And then, there are times when they remember or forget the things they did and thought when they were younger. There are plenty of things that can be vividly recalled, but capturing the tone, the essence of those times—it might be hard to recreate that in your mind. The sounds, the colors of those days feel like they belonged to that time alone.

The innocence of little children eventually fades away. The fighting spirit of dads is something unique to their own age. The carefree attitude of grandparents is something you only understand when you reach their age. Even within the same person, different tones emerge. I wouldn't say they become entirely different people, but it’s like they wear different paintings, different music on their bodies. Maybe that's why we can go on living without getting bored.
