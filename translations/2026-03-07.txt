Sunsets are lovely, but sunrises are truly the best.  
Shigesato Itoi

・I had the chance to meet with some elementary school students again.  
This time, it was at Shirakawa Elementary School, nestled at the foot of Mt. Akagi. Last November, we had a session called “”. Recently, they invited me to a sort of “presentation” that seemed like a continuation of that session.  
The sixth graders I spoke with back then are now on the brink of graduation. Just three months ago, they were worried about all sorts of things, saying things like “I’m anxious about growing up.” And now, they were holding a presentation to share what they’re thinking about these days.  
They wanted me to hear it, too. Naturally, I went. It felt like an invisible thread was connecting us.  
If I wrote about everything in detail, it would never end, so to put it simply, it was wonderful.

From the moment I arrived in the school parking lot to when I was guided to the waiting room, those sixth graders handled everything. And, to my surprise, they all spoke loudly and clearly. Back then, I told them that quiet voices can’t convey what you want to say, and that it would be a waste. It seems they took that to heart and changed right away!  
The teachers were amazed, and so was I.  
Each student spoke about their future. Some had role models nearby and were learning from them, others had begun to see what they loved and wanted to do, and some, though unsure of their future, were enjoying various things. They all shared honestly and happily.  
Even though they talked about dreams, they also mentioned practical conditions like “earning money,” “having days off,” and “finishing work on time,” which added a touch of realism that I found oddly reassuring.  
Most importantly, the anxieties and worries they were so eager to talk about had vanished. After their presentations, maybe from relief, they all broke into smiles.  
I couldn’t help but think, “Way to go!” It was the best.  
Go forth, walk, run, stumble, rest, slack off, live!  
For now, just being healthy and doing your best is a perfect score.  
Enjoy the tomorrow after tomorrow that I won’t see.

Thank you for visiting “Almost Daily” again today.  
I joke about it, but I’d love to invite you all to my funeral someday.
