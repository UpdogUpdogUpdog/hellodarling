Good things are good. And those that aren't...

By Shigesato Itoi

・When someone recommends a movie, or when I try a dish that someone raved about, and I find something delightful or tasty, I naturally want to share it with others. I might say, "This part was fascinating," or "It tasted like this," or "I made this discovery." Having been an advertising copywriter, I've always enjoyed contemplating what makes something "good" and maybe, I'm somewhat skilled at it. Sometimes, what might seem like a flaw can turn into a charming feature. With a change in perspective or angle, there's a lot to find interesting.

On the flip side, when I feel something is "not so great" or "not very good," I have to be careful about expressing that to others. Sometimes, my perception might just be off, or perhaps making me feel that way was part of the intention all along. I think there are many times when I simply can't find the "goodness" in something. Also, pointing out the "not-so-good" aspects can come quite naturally (laughs). People have this tendency to feel superior by picking out the faults in others. "Why do you see the speck in your brother's eye, but fail to notice the beam in your own?"—it's something that's been said since biblical times. With those thoughts in mind, I've decided that when I find something "not very good," I'll refrain from voicing it too much.

However, it's a bit tricky because "not saying anything" can mean both "choosing not to speak" and "I haven't seen or tried it," yet they both express the same silence. It's obvious, but it's true. I suppose this is how I'll continue to approach things.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" today. Finding "goodness" in things can be quite a pleasure in itself.
