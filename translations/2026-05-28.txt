In the dead of night, I buy tickets. That's me.

There's a term, "impulse buying," and maybe what I do falls under that category. I'm pretty much the kind of person who buys tickets on a whim. Even if it's an event I've never had any familiarity with, if something tells me I need to see it, I'm drawn to go. Even back in the days before the internet, I found myself attending all sorts of performances (and games). Like "Murata Choji's retirement game at Kawasaki Stadium," "Misora Hibari's Phoenix Concert at Tokyo Dome," "Pink Floyd at Hakone Aphrodite," "Sadaharu Oh's 756th home run game," or "RC Succession's debut at Hibiya Open-Air Concert Hall," I felt an abrupt urge to be there and went alone. It's what you might call a curiosity-driven impulse. I've got plenty more stories to boast about "seeing this or that," but I’ll stop before it turns into an old man's tales of the past.

Nowadays, with the ease of buying tickets online, this "I have to see it" feeling has become even more accessible and has grown healthily.

I also bought tickets for Kiyoshi Maekawa's concert alone in the middle of the night, and after twenty years, I was discovered in the audience and reunited with him. Yesterday's "Sayuri Ishikawa Pre-55th Anniversary Concert" was another ticket I bought with a late-night click. Apparently, Sayuri Ishikawa is quite close with Akiko Yano, and they’ve watched baseball together in America. I've also happened to sit next to Michiko Shimizu at a performance and exchanged greetings. But essentially, she's the person whose songs I know from karaoke, the one who brought classic pop hits into the world. Yet, realizing I'd never seen Sayuri Ishikawa live, I thought it would be a real shame to miss out. So, seizing the day, I made the reservation and headed to NHK Hall with Y-shita-san, who I'm not all that close with. (A large portion omitted) Wow, the singing was incredible, the voice, astounding. I was profoundly grateful to my late-night self for quietly purchasing that ticket.

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" today. You won't get another chance if you miss out on live events. Impulse is the best!
