Mr. Volume Zone.  
Shigesato Itoi

They call me "Mr. Volume Zone" around the "Hobo Nikkan" office. It’s because I've never quite managed to win this game we've been playing for ages now. The game is simple: each participant picks the smallest number they can think of, and if you're the only one to choose that number, you win the prize. The smallest number is obviously 1, followed by 2, and so on. But if more than one person picks the same number, you’re out. Everyone tries to think of a "small number that no one else would choose," but often, it turns out that several people have the same idea. That’s a total miss, and we all have a laugh about how off-mark we were. This collective wrong guess is what we call the "Volume Zone." It’s a gathering of the utterly ordinary, perhaps those lacking imagination. And quite often, I find myself right there in that group.

I always think I’m being serious about it. I go back and forth, determined that today will be the day I finally win. I want to be the sole victor, the one who stands out, but no matter how earnest, serious, or brave I get, I always miss the mark. I end up in the group with everyone else.

I’ve grown used to the laughter and even the consolation words that follow. "You’re in the 'Volume Zone,' that’s why your work goes well," they say. Apparently, I naturally think like the "majority." But is that really true? I mean, the things I usually do don’t always align with what most people naturally think, do they? I've been told my ideas are somewhat unique or new on more than one occasion. That’s probably why I’ve managed to make a career out of it, right? So where does that part of me exist, and who is this Mr. Volume Zone version of myself?

I often call myself "nothing special," and I genuinely believe that. But maybe the "secret" or "key" lies hidden somewhere in this paradox.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan" today. But on the flip side, what kind of person is really good at this game?
