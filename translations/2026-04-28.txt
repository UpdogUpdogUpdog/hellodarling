Olivia, it won’t be a big deal.  
Shigesato Itoi

・Now then.  
Ears—are they hair? No, they’re not. The area behind the ears—is that hair? No, it’s not.  
The nape of the neck—no matter how much allure it exudes, it’s not hair.  
The neck isn’t hair, nor is the forehead.  
So, I don’t plan on washing them with shampoo meant for hair.  
That said, I do end up washing the hairline on my forehead and the area behind my ears with shampoo, as part of the usual routine.  
I suppose everyone does that.

Hair is washed with shampoo, the face with foaming cleansers,  
and the rest of the body with body wash.  
We do it this way because it seems better.  
However.  
Would it be a catastrophe if you washed your face with shampoo,  
or used it on various parts of the body like the chest or stomach?  
Probably not a big deal.  
In fact, even if you used it to wash clothes or clean the bathtub,  
it wouldn’t be disastrous.  
It might sound like I’m fussing over trivialities,  
but isn’t that just how it is, folks?  
We use shampoos, facial cleansers, and body washes separately because they’re labeled that way.  
But even if you don’t strictly adhere to those divisions,  
generally, nothing catastrophic is going to happen.

This time, I had already decided what I wanted to write about—a rarity for me.  
I wanted to convey that “it’s not going to be a big deal.”  
That’s why I brought up the topic of shampoo.  
There are indeed plenty of truly serious matters out there.  
Things that involve life itself, or events that shake the world.  
I can’t speak to those; I don’t know about them.  
But when it comes to minor matters like what you wash with shampoo,  
there are countless things that aren’t a big deal.  
And it’s perfectly fine to let those slide, even if you strike out because of it.  
Honestly, even if you don’t read this piece,  
of course! It won’t be a big deal at all.

Thank you for visiting “Almost Daily” today.  
Even if it’s not a big deal, there are still things we want to hold dear.
