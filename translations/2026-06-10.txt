No dog exists that doesn't play.

Our dog, Vuiko, isn't exactly what you'd call energetic for a dog. Don't get me wrong, she's incredibly adorable, but "active" just isn't the word I'd use. She seems to enjoy being outside, but she's not a fan of walks. She'll wander around our yard for a bit and then quickly give me that look that says, "Can we go back now?"

Our previous dog, Vuillon, was the complete opposite. No matter how long the walk, she never once said "that's enough." It was always "more!" Even at home, she was constantly on the move, always ready to play. She'd bring a ball and drop it in front of me, demanding I throw it for her (Iwata-san often got roped into this). She'd race after it, come back at full speed, drop it again, and repeat the whole process over and over. Physical activity was her delight, play was her life. With our current stamina, there's no way we could keep up with her now.

It's not like we specifically chose our second dog to suit our current lifestyle, but Vuiko's laid-back nature is quite a relief. She only gets active when she's doing her "reporting" job, barking energetically to let us know someone's at the door. She always does a good job, and we're grateful for that.

Now, even our laid-back second dog occasionally invites me to play. She'll come into my line of sight and softly murmur a "vu." When I respond by standing up, she anticipates me by heading to where her toys are, picks up a rubber ball, and places it on the floor. I throw it, she goes after it, but she doesn't bring it back. Instead, she waits for me to come over to her. There's a departure, but no return. So, I make my way over to her. She just waits there.

I pick up the ball from where she stopped and throw it again. She chases after it, only to stop once she reaches it. After a few rounds of this, she stops chasing altogether—odd behavior for a dog. And with that, playtime wraps up in just a few minutes. It's fascinating that she doesn't completely reject playing, though. I guess, when all is said and done, she is a dog at heart.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" again today. Vuillon, Vuiko, and I—all of us need play in our lives.
