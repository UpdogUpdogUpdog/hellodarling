Sharing Disappointment with Someone  
Shigesato Itoi

・Yesterday, while browsing Twitter (X), I stumbled upon a post that read, "The zoo was closed today. I was so sad I cried." It prompted me to reply, "I recall several experiences like that. Sharing disappointment with my child has, in a way, become a cherished memory." And it's true. I wasn't the most exemplary father, but I did take my daughter to various places.

Back in those days, before the internet, there wasn't this constant need to make reservations for everything. We'd set out eagerly only to find the place was closed, or it was so crowded we had to turn back. Sometimes, we'd go fishing and catch nothing, or my daughter would fall ill during a trip. Yet, now when I look back, I'm grateful for those moments we experienced disappointment together. Thankfully, none of these moments involved serious injuries or accidents, but all the smaller misadventures and strokes of bad luck have turned into fond memories. Sure, sharing laughter and excitement is wonderful, but sharing letdowns and tears forms truly precious memories of "you and me."

The idea of "sharing disappointment" came to me as a parent-child thing, but it’s also vital in relationships with partners, friends, or coworkers. Of course, we don’t seek out disappointment or failure, but we can't avoid them either. Joy, anger, sorrow, happiness, and even feelings of failure are just a part of life. The blessing lies in having someone with whom you can reminisce about those "frustrating" moments.

Oh, perhaps the greatest joy comes from the lasting "experiences" and "relationships" they create. I'm just realizing this now, so I can't quite articulate it well.
