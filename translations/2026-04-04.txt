I managed to break free from my smartphone addiction in no time.
Shigesato Itoi

・Just yesterday, I wrote here about how the "magic box that holds everything = smartphone" has become so incredible that I can't let go of it. I've been relying on it so much (a dependency) that I need to think about a little "withdrawal." I'm not planning to throw away this extraordinary "magic box," but I do want to learn how to maintain a "healthy distance" with it. How do I figure out what that appropriate "distance" is? On the very day this piece got published on "Hobonichi," it seemed like my iPhone's communication function broke, and suddenly, I couldn't stay in touch while on the move.

I was shocked—completely clueless at first as to what wasn't working, so I tried various things. I realized I couldn't input or send out new information. The screen was perfectly visible and readable, but nothing was updating, which was a problem. No SUICA? What would happen to my e-tickets? (Spare you the tedious details of the whole saga... "It's fixed now, by the way").

Honestly, when I was ironically talking about "being dependent on my smartphone," I never imagined it would "break." When I thought about putting it in a safe for a while to distance myself, I took for granted that I could use it again when the time came. Planning to use it in moderation to avoid over-dependence, I was under the assumption that it would always be there.

They say, "Don't assume your parents or money will be there forever," but you know, the same applies to oil and smartphones. With the "magic box = smartphone," even when it's not just about the battery dying, there were always chances it could become "unusable." You might lose it somewhere, or it could get run over by a car if you dropped it.

I really need to reconsider this, the sense of distance. Like kids making promises to their parents, I'll think about imposing self-limits on how many hours a day I use it. It might be similar to stopping overeating and keeping your appetite at 80% full.

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" again today. But hey (chuckle), don't stop coming to "Hobonichi," alright?
