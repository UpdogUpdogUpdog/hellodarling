Gazing at my notes.  
Shigesato Itoi

・I have a note that reads, "A park where children are playing first."  
  Perhaps with some tidying up, it could be expressed differently,  
  but without much tinkering, I hold this note dear.

  There are children, already at play.  
  No need to specify what they're doing—  
  maybe skipping, maybe just looking around.  
  If their faces seem joyful,  
  someone might join in.  
  Even without knowing what they're up to,  
  saying "Let's play together" is enough, isn't it?  
  Maybe there's a little discussion of "What shall we play?"  
  But if it's puppies we're talking about, they just start sniffing around or chasing each other without any such talk.

  When one child becomes two,  
  the play becomes even more fun.  
  Seeing this, another child might join.  
  And then one by one, the circle of play expands.  
  The park becomes lively, brimming with life.

  I suppose it doesn't even have to be a place called a park—  
  just a "space" is enough.  
  When someone is "already playing," that's where it begins.  
  With a "space" and someone "already playing,"  
  there's a "future (the next moment)" there.  
  I guess I believe in that.  
  It feels like that's all I've ever done.

  Even before the play has a name, it exists.  
  The exchanges of "Let's play" and "Play with me"  
  enrich the play itself and the space where it happens.  
  The "space" for play is born, ages, and disappears,  
  but whenever someone starts playing there again, it is reborn.

  Don't stop the play.  
  Play is the future, always ready to begin anew.

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" again today.  
Even on days without good news, there are children already playing.
