The people who stand behind those who strive.
Shigesato Itoi

・The phrase "Thanks to everyone" is often used. It’s even been the title of a variety show, and we frequently hear or read it as a common expression. But there are many times when it’s spoken with genuine feeling. In fact, when I say it, I mean it wholeheartedly. Truly, from the bottom of my heart, it’s "thanks to everyone."

No one can live without the help of others. Even I, in my younger days, often thought, "I did this myself." I don't completely dismiss that youthful confidence, but at the same time, I feel like giving my younger self a lecture: "That’s not how it works." There were countless times when it was "thanks to everyone," and I’m sure I also caused plenty of "trouble for everyone." This isn't just a moral tale; it’s a fundamental truth.

For a clear example, think about a professional baseball manager. Even if he steps up to bat, he can’t hit the opposing pitcher’s ball. He can’t hit better than any of his players, and he’d be terrible at pitching or fielding—he wouldn’t even make it into the game. No matter who you are, there’s only a little you can accomplish on your own. Everything beyond that little bit is "thanks to everyone." From the moment we’re born with that first cry, a baby survives solely "thanks to everyone." We can flush the toilet because the sewer system works, and when our life ends, there’s someone to cremate us. The list goes on and on—it's everything!

Tomorrow, Monday, marks the start of a week-long "Life’s Pleasures Exhibition," and behind us, working as hard as we can, are our families and many others who support us. While fathers and mothers are striving, children wait patiently, and spouses offer their support. It’s because of this "thanks to everyone" that we can sweat and toil. I truly feel grateful for that.

Alright, it’s the final day of preparation. Let’s go with "Safety first, fun second!"
