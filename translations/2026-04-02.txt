The Gourmet Show with Matsuko Deluxe  
Shigesato Itoi

In so-called gourmet shows, there's an unspoken rule that saying "delicious" is taboo. The idea is to communicate about the food or sweets without using that word. Over time, this has led to the development of a language filled with descriptive phrases. Words like "soft," "sweet," "it's got a savory kick amidst the spiciness," or "just the right balance" have evolved as symbols of praise. However, as these symbolic expressions became more convenient, they started to lose their significance. Viewers now almost wish the personalities would just shout "delicious" from the heart. They long for the taste to be conveyed through the individual's personality and emotional response. Yet, few talents can express this in their own words, which is why most rely on these generic phrases to get by.

But recently, I was truly impressed by Matsuko Deluxe. It happened during a segment where she was about to eat a muffin. Matsuko often declares what she likes or craves before she eats, setting the stage with comments like, "I can't resist this," or "Hurry, let me eat!" She then takes a bite, hinting at her conclusion through her expressions and gestures, finally exclaiming something like, "What is this?!" in praise. However, with the muffin, she skipped the usual buildup and took a bite with a perplexed expression, chewing silently. I had the feeling Matsuko never particularly craved baked goods like muffins. Plus, muffins tend to dry out the mouth. She kept chewing with a stern face, creating a tense moment as she swallowed without saying a word. After a pause to catch her breath, she quietly whispered, "Incredibly delicious," as if sharing a secret. While relieved, I, as a viewer, was unsure how to interpret this reaction. Yet, the brief drama that unfolded was intriguing! Matsuko's artistry in expression is something neither inexperienced talents nor seasoned gourmet critics can replicate. She is an unmatchable presence—director, actor, and critic all in one.
