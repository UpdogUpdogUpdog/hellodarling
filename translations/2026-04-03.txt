In Just 20 Years, This Little Box

・Back in the day, whenever I planned a long vacation to a southern island, my suitcase was absurdly heavy. Naturally, I'd want to read while traveling, so I'd pick out some books. Even when I tried to limit myself, I'd still end up with over ten. Then there was the music—I’d pack several cassette tapes, which took up quite a bit of space. When CDs came along, it was no less troublesome. Plus, I needed something to play the music on. Whether it was cassettes or CDs, batteries were essential, so I'd bring those along too. Just with these items, my large suitcase was completely full. I'd squeeze in a swimsuit, sandals, and a few changes of clothes into the gaps, grunting my way to the airport. For a while, this set-up even included a heavy, bulky "personal computer."

Just reading this is exhausting, right? I find myself amazed that I actually did all that. And you know, all that hassle, except for the swim trunks and sandals, now fits into a tiny box. The once "heavy luggage" is entirely pocket-sized. That's right, it's what they call a "smartphone." Forget about ten or twenty books—you can carry a whole library with thousands of books. As for music, you could say every song known to man is inside this thing. Let's not forget to add a "camera" and a "wallet." And believe it or not, you can even fit a "movie theater" in there. No need to stock up on batteries, either. And get this, this "smartphone" can make calls to anywhere in the world. If you feel like writing a letter, you can do that too and send it instantly. The recipient can read it right away.

I used to fantasize about having such a magical device. But now there's something called a "smartphone" that even surpasses those fantasies. It's no surprise everyone desires it and becomes obsessed with it. In just 20 years, things have changed this much.

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" today. Just like weaning a child, we might need to think about "weaning" from our smartphones.
