Life is a bundle of thoughts in progress.  
Shigesato Itoi

・When all tasks and chores are finally done, I open my notebook to jot down my thoughts from the day. But when everything is truly finished, it’s already the edge of the night, a time when I should be going to bed. That’s when I realize how incredibly sleepy I am. I use a fountain pen to write in my Hobonichi Planner, but my handwriting is always at its worst. This has been a source of disappointment for me. Perhaps I should write in the planner first, then do my work or take a bath afterward. Yet, with too much free time, I find myself at a loss for what to write. This is something I need to reconsider.

・Every so often, books start overflowing in the house. I know my boss uses some sort of "simplified tidying method" to handle this chaos somehow. But when it becomes unmanageable, I find myself issuing a softer version of the "just get rid of them" command. A gentle voice suggests, "Perhaps I could gather the books that might be candidates for removal." It's troubling; it’s not an easy decision to make. Books I may or may not read create a landscape like stones along a riverbank, crafting a world between "books and me." I can’t declare any book as one I’ll never read again. For people like me, events where one can pass books on are essential. If the recipient is delighted, I’m happy to give them away. This, too, requires further contemplation.

・I own quite a few berets, so I decided to start wearing them. Wearing them almost daily has left a kink in my hair, which K, who cuts my hair, pointed out. It was suggested that perhaps I should wear hats less frequently. Come to think of it, it must be a pity for K to see their well-crafted haircut covered by a hat.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today. There are still countless trivial yet challenging puzzles to ponder.
