I'm a Margarine Ally.  
Shigesato Itoi

First off, let me just say this as a given: "Butter is delicious." I love butter, and there's no denying its deliciousness. At some point, I've even found myself preferring cakes with buttercream over those with fresh cream. The buttercream from the old days wasn't tasty, but today's buttercream stands proud and is utterly delicious. Since it's made from butter, of course, it's going to taste good.

With that baseline established, I want to stand up for margarine. Margarine has been mocked for ages. There was a brief period when people thought margarine, being plant-based, was healthier than butter's dairy fat, and it enjoyed a moment in the spotlight. But then, seemingly out of nowhere, it was hit with fierce criticism for containing a lot of "trans fats," and it fell out of favor. The truth is, margarine's production methods have been improved since then, and it seems that issue is no longer relevant. Yet, the notion that "margarine isn't good for you, right?" still lingers. When I learned this, I decided to rally behind margarine a bit, opting to spread it on my bread. It's easy to apply to toast and sandwiches, and besides, it's tasty in its own right. Hang in there, margarine. That's the sentiment I had.

Margarine can mimic butter, but butter can't imitate margarine. Plus, it spreads on bread like a dream. Butter isn't bad—it's quite good, actually—but margarine hasn't done anything to deserve dislike. With this mindset, I enjoy my daily toast. I support margarine, but that doesn't stop me from eating butter as well. I'm not taking sides; I don't want to create a divide in the world of spreads. Taste-wise, I may lean a bit toward butter, but I end up using more margarine. Somehow, this feels like a reflection of my personality.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan" again today. You could say I'm not straightforward, or you could say I'm just trying to find balance.
