Something Like Courage  
Shigesato Itoi

・When you do something because you want to, it outshines what you do out of obligation. Even if you're not trying to outdo anything, it naturally stands out. And even if it doesn't surpass, just wanting to do it means you've spent your time well.

Lately, I've been thinking about this a lot. I've pondered it before, but something like a small dash of courage was missing. I would suppress my desire for what I wanted to do and mix it with things I had to do.

I get it, there are things we must do. Maybe we've spent our whole lives practicing and executing these tasks. But truth be told, they don't stir the heart—not mine, nor probably anyone else's.

The other day, somewhere, I wrote jokingly, "I'll do it even if no one shows up," and it gave me a tremendous feeling of liberation. "I'll do it even if no one comes because I want to." It felt very much like courage. If you're always hoping for a full house, then just a little less than full can lead to disappointment and dissatisfaction. Wait, wasn't this something you wanted to do? If you genuinely believe "I'll do it even if no one shows up," you'll be delighted if even two or three people come. Plus, you got to do what you wanted.

Just being able to do what you want is already a huge achievement. The mere existence of such opportunities is extraordinary. And to these places, those meant to come will come.
