For those of us with no plans for the long holiday,  
by Shigesato Itoi

As April rolls around, even the most spontaneous among us can’t help but feel a little curious. Golden Week arrives whether we're planners or not. The prepared folks likely have everything mapped out already, but some of us are still plan-free. Take me, for instance. The thought of painstakingly making reservations, preparing for a trip, or navigating a packed schedule just doesn't appeal to me. Instead, I'll drift along, letting the weather guide my days. Adopting this approach is, in some ways, quite liberating.

That said, this year's holiday promises to be fulfilling. After all, Shibuya PARCO will become the place to be.

There were once "jazz cafés" and "rock cafés"—places where you could sip tea while enjoying music through an excellent sound system. I used to frequent such spots. But was there ever a "kayōkyoku café"? Probably not, because "kayōkyoku" was just "ordinary music" back then. You didn't need a special place to listen to it; it was everywhere—at home, on the streets. Nowadays, that scene is gone. Music has become something we experience individually, no matter where we are. While I understand and appreciate the intimacy of this, there's something incredibly joyful about feeling the sound waves envelop you in a shared space, surrounded by others who are also listening.

And when it comes to "kayōkyoku," which includes pop songs that capture the spirit of the times, it's simply wonderful(!). As for how the songs, the lyrics, and the music resonate, well, I'm sure someone will speak about it with "tremendous love." I'll be playing the role of a guest, but more often than not, I plan to be a regular attendee, like a customer at a favorite café. It’s a place to listen to kayōkyoku in excellent sound, free from applause or cheers. It’s an event that goes on every day, regardless of who shows up.

Thanks for stopping by "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" today. It feels like gathering after school in a classroom to listen to music together.
