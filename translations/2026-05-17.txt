Those Remarkably Resilient People I Admire  
by Shigesato Itoi

・There are things that are blatantly obvious, yet fundamentally crucial. Take, for instance, something I've mentioned several times before: Takumi Yoshimoto’s reason for his interest in Shinran. He once explained, “It largely stems from being raised in a family deeply devoted to Jodo Shinshu Buddhism.” Similarly, when asked, "Why is the TV always on?" he replied, "Isn't it because of loneliness?" Such answers might disappoint interviewers because they lack a certain sophistication. However, these are discussions rooted in fundamental truths.

In a similar vein, after witnessing Kenji Ozawa's incredibly energetic live performance, I found myself fixated on that vibrant energy. I understand what he's aiming for and what he wants people to feel. Yet, in the world of trees, that energy is like the flowers and the fruit. I wholeheartedly support people discussing the magnificence of those blossoms and fruits. But for me, I felt drawn to the foundational force—the roots and the trunk—that brings those flowers and fruits to life. Unapologetically, and perhaps rudely, I thought, "That guy is seriously robust!" He might appear like a delicate young man, but in reality, he harbors a fierce amount of energy. It reminds me of Seiji Ozawa, who, I suspect, was also brimming with immense energy.

The truly remarkable people in this world possess incredible stamina. I imagine even politicians must be like that. When someone lacks physical strength, they might exhaust every bit of their energy until they can give no more, ultimately closing the chapter of their life.

Yesterday, I attended a grand live performance by "Reiwa Roman" and the energy they unleashed in their comedy was nothing short of explosive. It's people like that who captivate audiences. As for me, I'm weak, low-energy, and honestly, it's pathetic.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shimbun" today. I hope to find a way to remain interesting, even in my feeble state.
