This text isn't etched onto parchment.  
Shigesato Itoi

・Right now, as I'm writing this,  
  it's not because it's of great importance.  
  I'm aware there are countless things that matter more.  
  Yet, even things that aren't crucial sometimes need to be said.  
  It's amidst all those unspoken truths  
  that I find myself writing these words.  
  For every thought that's put into words,  
  there are billions left unsaid.  
  So why, out of everything, am I writing this?  
  If you asked me seriously, I wouldn't have a proper answer.

  If there were something I truly wanted to write,  
  I'd certainly write it, though I can't say for sure  
  if it would be something truly significant.  
  Perhaps it's worth saying that not only important things matter.  
  Maybe this is one of those seemingly unimportant yet important things.  
  It's all a bit complicated, isn't it?  
  I sometimes think I should have started with something simpler,  
  but it's too much trouble to change course now, so I'll carry on.

  One reason I can write about something seemingly pointless like this  
  is because this medium isn't stone tablets or parchment.  
  In ancient times, writing was done on precious materials.  
  Even paper, which came much later, was valuable.  
  To share something with others, it had to be printed and distributed.  
  Now, without bearing those "high costs,"  
  we can freely write "oh really" or "wow" whenever we please.  
  The meaning of value, the calculation of costs, the weight of words,  
  and the qualifications for writing have all transformed.  
  We're rethinking what it takes to write and communicate.  
  The meaning of knowledge and education has also shifted.  
  It's intriguing to ponder what that means, isn't it?
