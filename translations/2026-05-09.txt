Longing for Hope

By Shigesato Itoi

・Perhaps most people are in search of hope. They might not explicitly state, "I want hope," but the desire is there. To be honest, I’ve never uttered the words, "I want hope," myself. It was more of a quiet realization, a feeling nestled deep within my heart.

Every day, we are bombarded with a relentless stream of news. A lot of it is negative. And, since it's often true, we must first accept it. But understanding what to do about it is not easy.

We're constantly exposed to opinions about what's happening now or predictions about the future. We learn about these things, but they rarely inspire joy.

Suddenly, I realized, "Wait a minute, where is hope in all of this?" It struck me—how little of what we hear, learn, and ponder leads to hope.

If asked whether there was more hope in the past, I’d say, perhaps it was always hard to find. Yet, seeing a middle school student working earnestly or encountering people who seemed hopeful, there was hope there. Maybe, just maybe, people who carry hope are the embodiment of it. Even when the world tries to convince them of its bleakness, hopeful people remain hopeful.

Sure, there are charlatans who exploit hope, those who crush it, and even those who mock it. But knowing this doesn’t make hope disappear. Hope doesn’t need justification or guarantees. The first cry of a newborn, that "waah," was pure hope in itself. I’ve decided to continue seeking hope anew.
