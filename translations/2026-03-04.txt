The Beret People, the Cold People, and the Glasses People  
Shigesato Itoi

・“You've been wearing a beret lately, is it because of, you know, that connection?” someone asked me.  
Sure, it's true that many cartoonists, like Osamu Tezuka or Fujiko Fujio A, seem to be beret people (beret-bito).  
I might have been inspired by them, but that's not really it.  
I've worn a beret from time to time before.  
I don’t wear hats often, but I've tried on various kinds.  
Take the flat caps from "Peaky Blinders"—I’ve bought several online, rejecting one after another, trying to find the right one.  
Though, honestly, I almost never wear them.  
And as for berets, well, I already owned a few.  
Then one day, I just thought, "Why not wear one?"  
That led to buying even more, wanting to choose from different styles, and now I wear one every day.  
I've decided to keep this up until I get tired of it (but that's enough about that—now, let me share something truly practical).

How to avoid catching a cold and keep your glasses from fogging up!  
It's like killing two birds with one stone, right? Impressive, isn't it?

First, to avoid catching a cold, handwashing is key.  
It's a well-known fact that doctors, despite seeing countless cold-ridden patients (kaze-bito), don’t catch colds themselves because they wash their hands frequently.  
So, I try to follow their example.  
These days, there's foaming hand soap that makes it incredibly easy to wash your hands frequently.  
Now, if you don’t wear glasses, you can stop here, but for my fellow glasses-wearers (megane-bito), listen up.  
When you're washing your hands, use the foam to clean your glasses too.  
Just lather up the lenses and the frames, and rinse with water—avoid hot water, as it can damage the lenses.  
Gently shake off the excess water.  
A single tissue will absorb the remaining droplets.  
It's simpler and more effective than any other cleaning method.  
You might find it so easy that it becomes a habit.
