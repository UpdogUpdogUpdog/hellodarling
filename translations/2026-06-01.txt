Age doesn’t matter here.  
Shigesato Itoi

・Here we go, it’s live time. Live means alive, raw, vibrant.  
  In the concrete setting of the Shinjuku Sumitomo Building Triangle Plaza,  
  with real shops lined up and real people gathering,  
  the live event is about to commence.  
  It's not virtual or digital at all.  
  Sure, we get some help from digital tech,  
  but everything happening in the moment is live.

  From the preparation stage, the team worked pretty hard,  
  and now they're buzzing with excitement, praising each other.  
  I totally get that.  
  "Is it that we’re getting the hang of it?" the veterans muse.  
  Sure, everyone has their worries,  
  but somehow, everyone else seems reliable.  
  That’s fine, isn’t it?  
  We start with the feeling that we’ve done everything we can.  
  What happens will happen, so let’s keep this momentum going.

  The event has already begun even before the official start.  
  Just like last time, the two-person team is going all out.  
  It dawned on me again that  
  these two have an age gap wider than a parent and child.  
  Yet, you don’t feel that at all.  
  They're senior and junior, but age doesn’t matter.  
  This becomes apparent when you read the "text relay,"  
  where everyone featured, regardless of age, just fits in.  
  Whether you’re young or old, it doesn’t matter.  
  You really notice this when doing something as tangible as  
  the "Living Enjoyment Exhibition."  
  It seems like age doesn’t exist for everyone at "Hobo Nichi."  
  Plus, there’s no one flaunting their seniority.  
  I find this environment incredibly comfortable.  
  For someone like me, an old-timer, to not be pitied or shunned,  
  but to be included in the fun, is truly a blessing.  
  Speaking of which, the current "Living Enjoyment Exhibition"  
  is spearheaded by people in their twenties.  
  Isn’t that fantastic? I love a company like this.  
  And so, with a cheerful heart, we are ready to embrace day one.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today.  
I’m slightly excited as I face this day. "Hobo Nichi" is the best.
