Soaking in a dimly lit bath, I close my eyes.

Whether it helps you sleep better or not is beside the point; just lying down with your eyes closed is enough. I've heard that countless times, but when someone else says it, it hits me anew.

Today unfolded beautifully, a curtain rising on a day that was busy for everyone yet mostly serene. Of course, there are things that need consideration and tasks I'm not great at, but I want to embrace even those. Sleep deprivation isn't just my problem; it’s something that needs separate attention.

Lying back in a tepid bath with the lights off, eyes closed, feels undeniably good. I’m not asleep, but it’s awfully close. It must be a good thing, something important.

And then, inevitably, I start to wonder what makes this so pleasurable. Perhaps it’s the idea of being "unguarded." Most of the time, we’re all "guarding" ourselves. It's necessary, I suppose, but it certainly demands a lot of tension. Creating moments where we can let down these defenses and become "unguarded" is important. It’s much like sleeping soundly and feeling secure.

We adults love to see utter vulnerability. Whether it’s a baby or a young animal, they sleep with unparalleled ease. Realizing that we share this same world of infinite security brings joy. When worries, fears, doubts, and competitiveness slip away, I feel drawn to follow these fragile beings into their state of "unguarded" peace. Yes, that’s what I’ve needed every day. I must absorb "unguardedness" like a nutrient. Even just closing my eyes in the bath helps a little.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan" again today. I’m writing this after getting out of the bath, and oh, am I sleepy.
