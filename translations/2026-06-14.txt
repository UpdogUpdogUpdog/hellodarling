"The Unremarkable Person"
Shigesato Itoi

To truly be an "unremarkable person."  
To be an "unremarkable person" from the heart.  
For everyone to see through my foolishness,  
and know I'm nothing special.

No matter how much I try to exaggerate,  
no matter how hard I strive to show off,  
an "unremarkable person"  
remains an "unremarkable person."

Even with some effort or unnoticed struggles,  
an "unremarkable person"  
is still an "unremarkable person."  
A little kindness here, an occasional act of goodwill there—  
an "unremarkable person"  
stays unremarkable, no matter what.

Even with a gold medal, still "unremarkable."  
Even if called a great person, still "unremarkable."  
No matter what I do or achieve,  
since I've always been "unremarkable,"  
I remain so.

You, me, him, her—  
we were all born "unremarkable."  
Sometimes, we might get a bit "better,"  
or become a little "serious."

Being "unremarkable" doesn't mean losing anything or missing out.  
As an "unremarkable person,"  
I can climb high mountains and dive deep seas.  
Just as I am, I can laugh, cry, rejoice,  
find happiness, and even achieve amazing things.  
There's no complaint in being "unremarkable."

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" again today.  
There's probably not a single person who isn't "unremarkable."
