Are you lost in the moment?

Shigesato Itoi

・Being completely absorbed in something is truly a wonderful thing. If you're currently engrossed in something, cherish that time—it's a treasure. But perhaps what's even more amazing is not even thinking about whether you're absorbed or not. When children are lost in play, it's like they become one with it. Even when told, "That's enough for now," they pay no heed to their parents' words.

Whether it's music, manga, games, or sports—honestly, it doesn't matter what it is.

I still vividly remember the night I found a book so captivating that I secretly read it until the early hours. It was "Dr. Mambo's Insect Diary" by Morio Kita. Staying up late as an elementary school kid is probably not great for one's health, and it could become a bad habit. Books can always wait until the next day, so it's best to use a bookmark and go to sleep. It's a guardian's role to teach that. Yet, the feeling I had at that time remains a treasure for me. The clock's short hand had moved to an unfamiliar place, and though I was in bed, I couldn't stop reading. I don't remember much else, but I doubt I finished the book that night. Midnight was probably no later than 1 AM. Still, that moment of being utterly absorbed was incredibly important to me.

As an adult, there have been times when I've been engrossed in games, dramas, or even work, losing track of time. I have a strong desire to embrace all of these experiences. My passion, my "being absorbed," has softened over the years.

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" today. Playing is better than enjoying, and enjoying is better than learning—even for adults.
