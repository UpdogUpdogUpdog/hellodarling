Itoi Shigesato Praised? More Like Overwhelmed.  
Shigesato Itoi

・Wow, it's been ages since I last felt like this.  
  I was utterly wiped out, like my head was spinning,  
  as if there was just not enough oxygen, and I was done for.  
  Why, you ask? Where on earth did this happen?  
  At the "Sanseido Jimbocho Main Store".  
  I mentioned this in yesterday's "Hobo Nikkan",  
  but during the preparation for the grand opening,  
  the crew from "Hobo Nikkan" was allowed a special visit.  
  Each of us got to pick one book to buy.  
  Thanks to our neighborhood connections,  
  we got to do something this delightful.

  I was aware of Sanseido's new store plans,  
  having attended some launch events,  
  but seeing it materialize as a real bookstore was astounding.  
  I'm being perfectly candid here, no need for flattery,  
  but there's a genuine "heartbeat" to both the plan and its execution.  
  It's like a movie where you'd want to list "executive producer",  
  "director", "art director", and so on in the credits.  
  Someone envisioned this, and everyone brought it to life together.  
  Recently, when you visit the Ichikawa City Zoo with Punch-kun the monkey,  
  you can sense the staff's creativity and care in crafting the place,  
  and it feels similar here.  
  It's as if the books themselves are saying,  
  "Where do I come from, and where do I want to go?"  
  This is thanks to the thoughtful curation and arrangement.  
  My eyes and my feet were drawn to the books,  
  wandering and engaging in conversation.  
  They aren't just randomly lined up,  
  so you feel compelled to give them proper attention.  
  That's why I got so exhausted.  
  It felt like having back-to-back meetings with countless individuals.  
  Curiosity, challenges, confessions, temptations—they never ceased.  
  In a way, it was like being lost in a "forest of distractions"  
  (I can't just call it a "forest of knowledge" or anything mundane).  
  I was truly overwhelmed; I should have been more prepared.  
  So, next time, I'll visit as a regular customer and try again.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan" today.  
I've made a note of all the books I'll buy on my next visit.
