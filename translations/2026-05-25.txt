It might not be worth it.

Shigesato Itoi

・The Korean drama on Netflix, "Everyone Battles Their Own Worthlessness," is finally wrapping up with its 12th episode. At this moment, I've watched up to episode 11, and I wonder if they can truly conclude it in just one more episode (an hour and five minutes, I think). Will it end with a sense of "and the story continues"? Either way, I've been completely drawn in by this drama. I suspect many will watch it after me, so I won't spoil too much, but I must say Park Hae-young’s script is simply outstanding.

Perhaps part of the allure lies in the fact that the story is about people who make movies. These individuals pour their passion into filmmaking, weaving together their dreams and emotions as they go about their lives. Of course, they want to create a hit and succeed in the business sense. Yet, without a deeper passion and seriousness, this story would never stand. That’s what makes it so compelling. There are limits to what you can achieve by simply weighing profit and loss. So much cannot be done under the mindset of "labor for compensation." Why, then, do these characters become so engrossed in something that doesn't necessarily reward them? The answer isn't directly spelled out in the film. However, without people like this, not just movies but all forms of expression might never be polished or preserved.

Speaking of which, Kenji Ozawa recently mentioned about his stage performances, "Playing with younger people is truly inspiring." I found myself agreeing and also imagining how, even in today's world, young people are diligently practicing music and pursuing careers in performance. In a way, playing instruments at a high level isn't exactly a lucrative job if you start talking about cost-effectiveness. Yet, I felt delighted knowing that there are still "young people" who inspire Kenji Ozawa. The fact that there are still many who dive into such work—requiring countless skills and endless practice—beyond just "profit and loss" or "labor and reward" is truly a sign of hope.

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" again today. The joy of being able to do what you want to do is truly immense.
