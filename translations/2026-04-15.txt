Is the face of the person before me my mirror?

Shigesato Itoi

A well-experienced individual once said to me, "When you look at someone's face, you can see your own. If the other person seems unhappy, you have to assume you're wearing the same expression." I found that rather insightful. Of course, it depends on the situation, but thinking that way seems spot-on.

This reminds me of an old joke: "There was a monkey in a box that mimicked everything. The box had a hole, so people peeked inside to see what was happening, but they saw nothing. The monkey was peeking back at them from the other side." I suddenly recalled this and mused that such things happen quite often.

When driving a car at 50 kilometers per hour, the scenery in front of you is rushing toward you at the same speed. It's a bit unnerving when you think about it like that.

Speaking of which, there's a fable that goes something like this: "A traveler arrives at a village and asks the villagers what the place is like. The villagers ask in return about the traveler’s last village. He replies that it was a terrible place, full of unfriendly, grumpy people. Hearing this, the villagers nod and say, 'In that case, this village might be the same.'"

I also recall something Yoshimoto Takaaki once said: "When we naturally interact and change something, nature also exerts force back on us, causing change." For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

When you set 'this side and that side,' 'humans and nature,' 'others and oneself' against each other, they become action and reaction. But in reality, there might be more to say about how relationships are formed, whether they become one, create distance, or change their boundaries.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan" today. I hope that as you read this, you find yourself in good spirits.
