Perhaps the night is longer than the day.  
Shigesato Itoi

・It's not uncommon to compare a person's life to the path of the sun.  
  Many people seem to do this quite often.  
  In the beginning, it's like the sun rising from the horizon.  
  A newborn baby lights up the world around them,  
  and just watching them brings joy.

  Eventually, the sun climbs higher and higher.  
  The light grows stronger, and perhaps the sun seems larger.  
  It feels like an endless day.  
  Sometimes, the brightness can wither the grass and trees.  
  In the fiery days of youth, the help of water and wind is needed.

  But it's impossible for the day to last forever.  
  Somehow, the sun is no longer at its highest point.  
  It starts to descend toward the earth.  
  The brightness fades, and there's no more glare.  
  In the western sky, a gentle, rosy light quietly sinks.

  Is this the end of the sun's story? Not quite.  
  One might think the moon and stars take over for the sun,  
  but it's not so simple.  
  It's not that life's journey ends here, either.  
  Well, this is where night begins.  
  Surprisingly long, night stretches on, as long as day, long and longer.  
  You could easily drift off to sleep,  
  or stay awake as you would during the day.

  As you ponder what to do with this long night,  
  eventually, the sun returns from the eastern sky.  
  Oh, so it repeats.  
  Even if we liken a single day to a person's life, wait a minute,  
  there's no ending to this, is there?

  When I think about it, I've parted ways with friends I used to play with.  
  I've grown tired of and forgotten many things.  
  Yet, even at night, there are people to meet,  
  and the music keeps playing, inviting us to play.  
  After dusk, the night shines brightly.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today.  
Like a lullaby for someone, I'm singing whatever comes to mind.
