About Fairies and Such  
Shigesato Itoi

・Part One: "About Fairies"  
On this earth, there are people known as "fairies."  
They don't have wings fluttering like a hummingbird, nor do they sip nectar from flowers.  
They take human form and live among us, indistinguishable in appearance from anyone else.  
They aren't necessarily delicate young girls; often, they might appear as old women or middle-aged men.  
At first glance, there's nothing fairy-like about them, yet if you say, "Hey, that person looks like a fairy, don't they?" people around often nod in agreement.  
To repeat, they have no wings and look entirely human.

Yet, they are a kind-hearted breed, capable of a touch of mischief, with minds that harbor a clear blue sky, and hearts that occasionally dance with joy.  
They seem to stretch the day to 36 hours, or play hide-and-seek alone without inviting anyone, leaving us humans behind on the ground.  
I belong to those humans, and later realize, "Ah, the fairy flew into the sky."  
Indeed, they might have flown off without wings or could come tumbling down with some peculiar call.  
That's just how "fairies" are, and there's nothing to be done about it.

・Part Two: "And Such"  
Sometimes you visit a restaurant, and think, "I'd love to come back here."  
Conversely, there are places that live up to their reputation, and yet, you feel, "I probably won't come back."  
There was nothing wrong, and the food was delicious.  
But if you ask whether you'd return, the answer seems to be no.  
It's not just about restaurants—it's the same with people.  
There are those you feel, "We'll meet again, I want to see them."  
Even while you're with them, you sense you'll meet again.  
And then, there are those, though nothing is wrong, that you feel, "I probably won't see them again."

I can't quite pin down what's different between the two, but I've decided it doesn't matter.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today.  
I ended up writing this like a diary, without considering the reader.
