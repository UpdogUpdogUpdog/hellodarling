Power: A Reflection  
Shigesato Itoi

・Be it wealth, beauty, or some kind of talent,  
  it's all about power.  
  Most people wish for their power to grow.

  With greater power, perhaps,  
  comes more freedom.  
  More possibilities.  
  More happiness.  
  More friends.  
  So they imagine.

  Yet, with greater power, perhaps,  
  also comes less freedom.  
  More things you mustn't do.  
  More unhappiness.  
  More conflicts with friends.  
  Such thoughts are possible too.

  Becoming someone important means  
  you get to ride in a big car with bulletproof glass.  
  But why is there bulletproof glass?  
  It might be for that very reason.  
  Simply saying you don't want power is too easy.

  If you have almost no power, then, I wonder,  
  is there freedom?  
  Are there possibilities?  
  Is there happiness?  
  Are there friends?  
  Who knows, really.  
  Maybe there is, maybe there isn't.

  Even with all the history and countless stories we know,  
  the answer to this remains elusive.  
  Be it epic dramas or gangster films, it's the same.  
  Handling power is always the toughest question.  
  "Just the right amount of power" is hard to narrate,  
  and even in the ordinary, power must be lurking in the background.
