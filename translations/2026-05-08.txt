How's everyone doing? Feeling a bit off?

Shigesato Itoi

There's a term that's been around for ages: "May sickness." It's that time of year when you have new school terms starting, or you're settling into a new job in April. Winter's finally over, the temperature's rising, and everything's changing around you. Then, as if on cue (though it's not exactly a surprise), Golden Week rolls around. And just when the holidays end, you're thrust back into a "not quite settled" routine. It's no wonder that whether you're a kid or an adult, you end up feeling a bit out of sorts.

They say any kind of "change" is a source of stress. New environments, challenges—sure, those are obvious. But even positive changes can be stressful, apparently. At this rate, even the "clear skies of May" become a source of stress. When I think about it all, I can't help but chuckle: Is there anyone out there who's truly energetic in May?

How about you? Oh, me? Of course, I'm a mess—not that I'd call it "May sickness." When I chat with people around me, they all seem to grumble as if it's a universal truth: "May is just not a good month." It's funny how we bond over this shared sentiment.

I don’t know much about the Meiji or Edo periods, but I suspect that people in Japan have always struggled in May. If that's the case, you'd think there'd be some kind of wisdom or tradition to help us get through this rough patch. Oh, wait, there is: "Tango no Sekku." With illness spreading easily at the change of seasons, people would display warrior dolls or fly carp streamers to ward off evil spirits. I guess I got too caught up in the holidays and forgot the importance of these seasonal events. Now, the only "wisdom" I can muster is the idea that maybe I should just eat more kashiwa mochi.

Thanks for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today. Perhaps this grumbling captures the essence of my May.
