Places Where You Can Meet Someone You Love  
by Shigesato Itoi

・The place where you can meet someone you love is where they love to be.

So, if you know what your loved one enjoys, you'll figure out where to meet them. Sometimes, people with shared interests become acquainted and end up finding someone they love. It sounds like I’m describing the matchmaking rules of the internet age, but this line, "the place where you can meet someone you love," was actually a jingle I wrote for a commercial when I was around 30. It suddenly came back to me, and I found myself humming it.

Back in 1979, a Seiyu supermarket opened in front of Saga Station, and this was the theme for the campaign ad. I went to Saga for location scouting and interviews, but the locals kept saying, "There's nothing here." We thought, "But it's right in front of the station," yet it was true—there were hardly any people around. Occasionally, a biker gang would circle the station area. Still, many were optimistic, saying, "Once Seiyu opens, people will start coming little by little." When "something" (in this case, Seiyu) appears where there's "nothing," people will gather. That’s the logic behind the company's decision to build it, I suppose. With these thoughts in mind, I crafted the copy. I believe it was, "The place where you can meet someone you love." And this theme became the opening lyric of the commercial. Almost fifty years later, I can recall what I was thinking back then as if it were just yesterday. And I realize I'm still pondering the same things.

People's actions have always been the same, haven't they? The place where you can meet someone you love is where they love to be. Everything "Hobonichi" has done so far, what we recently opened, and what’s about to start is all the same. It’s not about sophisticated concepts like community or regional revitalization. It’s simply about wanting people who love each other to gather together.

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" again today. By the way, the "Saga Seiyu" closed its doors in 2018.
