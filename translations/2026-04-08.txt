Life is a Work in Progress  
Shigesato Itoi

・Just how many unfinished tasks do I have lying around?  
  Without pushing myself too hard, I've always been the type to just keep going with these "in-progress" things. It's like a habit of mine.

  Still, there are so many things left unfinished. It's not just work-related stuff. The books I want to read pile up endlessly near me. The "invisible hand" of my household seems to relocate them elsewhere in stacks, but then more books gather again in the "close" vicinity. I really do want to watch all those subscription dramas too. Honestly, I do. Yet, my lists are filled with half-watched shows and titles queued up in "My List."

  Fortunately, when it comes to events, the hassle of making reservations ensures I attend unless something truly inconvenient arises. So, in that sense, there’s nothing left unfinished there. As for deciding what to eat, unless it's something that requires a reservation, like ramen, all I do is accumulate information. I love the show "Eating Ramen" on BS-TBS. As for meeting people, now that I'm older, I try to remind myself, "I might not be able to see them again." I have to admit, it's also about me—"You might not get the chance to see me again," I think. But since I can't possibly meet everyone at any time, this too just keeps piling up.

  However, when I think about it more deeply, there's no such thing as "wrapping things up neatly before I die." From the moment we're born until we die, we're always in a state of "in-progress." Having a lot of unfinished business—does it mean I'm greedy, or simply living life to the fullest? It's not a bad thing at all, although it is quite exhausting.

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" today. But I did end with "it's quite exhausting," didn’t I? (laughs)
