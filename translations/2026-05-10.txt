I have a quiet little pursuit I've been working on.  
Shigesato Itoi

・I thought I’d keep it to myself and carry on in silence,  
  but I couldn’t resist sharing it any longer.

I've bought a brand new pair of jeans, and my plan is to wear them relentlessly until they become those perfectly broken-in jeans. Not the pre-distressed ones or vintage ones worn by someone else. I want to shape them to my own liking. Back when I first started "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun," I took this endeavor quite seriously. I managed to get them to that "just right" state, but with all the desk work, the seat area inevitably wore too thin. I don't remember all the details from back then, but I think I wore them for almost a year. Some folks are so committed they don’t even wash them, but being a working adult, I did wash mine while wearing them regularly.

It's been over a quarter of a century since those days. I own a lot of jeans now, so I haven’t committed to wearing just one pair consistently. But when I decided to start fresh, it turned out to be quite a challenge and required a lot of conscious effort. It's not that I’m aiming for a fashion statement or trying to be stubborn by wearing the same jeans every day. It’s just something I felt like doing. It might be akin to deciding to take up fishing or running. In a somewhat philosophical sense, the daily accumulation of time records itself in the creases, fades, and wear of a single pair of jeans. Nobody else can buy or create the same thing. It's funny that at my age, I find myself wanting to take on such a project. I've been wearing them for about three months now. There’s a change, almost imperceptible to others. It’s at that stage where you wonder if they’re starting to get that "just right" look. If I’m honest, I’m already bored of it, but having written about it today, I feel like I can keep going.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" today. I think I might just be suited for simple, repetitive tasks.
