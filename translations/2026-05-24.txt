Is there anyone who seems truly happy?

Is there someone who strikes you as genuinely happy? It could be someone close to you, or even yourself. It might be a famous person you've never met. Ideally, it shouldn't be someone imaginary. It would be nice if it were someone you could actually meet. I find myself wanting to meet people who others perceive as happy and listen to their stories. I haven't planned anything concrete yet; it just came to me out of the blue.

Yesterday, I went to see "The Devil Wears Prada 2." The movie was entertaining and quite successful. Even the original from twenty years ago is still enjoyable today, and making a sequel with the same cast feels like a high-wire act. Yet, the fact that they pulled off such a production is truly impressive. It's not just a film that's stylish and cheerful, but the dazzling costumes add a clever touch. As I walked home alone, I pondered, why doesn't this movie feel like a happy ending? Every character is earnest and wise in their own way, but to me, none of them seemed genuinely happy. Even though they achieve victories and success, including the protagonist, no one struck me as "happy."

So, I asked myself: Where are the people I perceive as happy? Who are the people that everyone thinks seem happy? Are they presidents, idols, or athletes? Among those I know, who do I consider to be happy? Happiness is subjective, after all, and the more I think about it, the more tangled it becomes. If that's the case, could I meet someone perceived as happy and ask them about their life? It's still just an idea I had on my own, but I plan to discuss it further with others as a possible project.
