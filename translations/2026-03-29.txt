The Gift of Being Accepted  
by Shigesato Itoi

Around the time I was nearing thirty, I remember saying something like this: "We often hear the term 'delinquent,' but it's really more like being 'lost.' Not knowing which path to take, where to belong, they're just lost." Whether or not this is accurate etymologically, it struck a chord with me and has stayed in my mind ever since.

If you have even one place to return to, one destination, in other words, if you're not truly lost, there are many stories of people finding their way back from there. I've heard that when a troubled youth feels rejected everywhere, sometimes the only welcoming arms are those of a truly bad crowd. To be "accepted" is an extraordinary thing.

The world is full of various "flocks of people." Each has its merits and quirks, and yes, some do engage in genuinely bad behavior. If you find yourself in a group that feels foreign, you might think, "Everyone's just talking about ◯◯, and it was tough for me." But within that group, talking about ◯◯ might be the most natural thing in the world.

They say "birds of a feather flock together," but within those flocks, there’s a mutual acceptance, isn’t there? Where can I, this bird, be accepted? What kind of bird will this flock accept? Perhaps such negotiations happen more often than we realize. I've lived without giving it much thought, but looking back, I've been welcomed by many people. I genuinely feel grateful for that. It means I haven't been rejected, and even that alone is something to be thankful for. Not being denied, having a place, even being understood—these things are as vital as air and water.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shimbun" today. When you have the assurance of being accepted, you can stand alone with confidence.
