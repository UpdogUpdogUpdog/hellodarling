My Thoughts on the Deliciousness of Rice  
Shigesato Itoi

・I won't claim that all sushi rice is perfect, but let's be honest, most of it is pretty delicious. The touch of vinegar and a hint of sugar certainly play a role in its appeal, but there's something magical about each individual grain of rice. Every grain is smooth, distinct, and stands on its own. You can truly appreciate that rice is the "seed of the grass" we call rice plants.

Sushi rice is essentially cooked rice coated with "sushi vinegar." A crucial part of this process is cooling the rice by fanning it with something like a hand fan. This lowers the temperature and evaporates the steam. By doing so, the surface moisture is removed, preventing the rice from becoming mushy. I know this sounds like a hassle, but it's important to grasp this first. While cooled and dried sushi rice is delicious, the very act of keeping rice warm makes its surface sticky. When you keep cooked rice warm, the steam has nowhere to escape, and it condenses back onto the rice grains, making them soft and soggy. That's the crux of the problem, and I hope you feel it as keenly as I do.

There are countless opinions and methods when it comes to cooking rice. I've experimented with many, but lately, I've settled on a simple conclusion: as long as you don't keep it warm, the rice will generally taste great. Transferring it to a wooden container is nice, and even non-wooden ones work well. Making rice balls is another good option, or after fluffing the freshly cooked rice, just cover it with a paper towel without sealing it completely. You could even let it cool in bowls. The charm of "warm rice" is truly limited to freshly cooked rice. If you're going to let it sit, skip the warming and enjoy it cold. If you want it hot, reheating it in the microwave is the way to go. I realize I might sound overly passionate about this, but here I am, sharing what I'd tell a friend.

Thank you for visiting "Hobonichi" today. Sushi and rice balls taste wonderful even when they've cooled down, as they should.
