"Not Quite a 'Culture Festival,' You Know."  
by Shigesato Itoi

・The moment is almost upon us.  
There's a palpable sense of anticipation in the office at "Hobo Nichi."  
Everyone's cranking up their gears just a notch.

Sure, the "Living Pleasure Exhibition" is work, but it feels a bit like a "working vacation" for employees.  
Though, if I put it that way, some might recoil at the idea, thinking, "That sounds terrible."  
And yes, if it were a "forced working vacation," it might indeed be dreadful.  
I can't quite articulate it well, but people often compare it to a "culture festival."  
Yet, calling it a "culture festival" seems to fall a bit short.  
While there are variations in skill and approach, what we're doing is, after all, still work.

Perhaps "a shared experience space" is a closer description.  
The crew at the office, those who've come to exhibit, the visitors who turn up, and the part-timers who join us all get to share this experience.  
What experience, you ask? Ah, that's the tricky part—I can't quite put it into words.  
It's all about feeling those sensations that defy the "trendy language" of today, exchanging and sharing them with one another.  
Eventually, everyone continues to discuss and reflect, turning these into memories, conversations, and methods.  
This becomes the wisdom of "next time, let's do it better."  
The feeling that we can do more next time becomes "hope."  
Afterward, we share our exhaustion and rest together.  
There's this bubbling urge to talk about everything with others.  
Then, we return to our regular jobs, the ones not tied to the "Living Pleasure Exhibition."  
New plans are slowly spoken into life, and the cycle towards the future begins anew.

Michael Polanyi's concept of "tacit knowledge" evolved into the "SECI" model,  
and perhaps what we're doing aligns with that.  
Or maybe we're just kicking around a ball labeled "I can't explain it well, but it's great," playing with it endlessly.  
We're not moving things according to theory, so as usual, we're bustling about before the main event.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today.  
I'm also gearing up to tackle that long marathon in my own way.
