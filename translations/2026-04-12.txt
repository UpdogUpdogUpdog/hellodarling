Your Amida Lottery and Mine  
By Shigesato Itoi

"How did you meet so-and-so?"  
I sometimes get asked this question out of the blue.  
Not during interviews or anything, but by someone nearby, like a colleague.  
Sometimes I can answer right away, but other times, I’m left scratching my head, unsure.  
I’ve spent time with this so-and-so in various settings.  
I can recall many shared moments, but where did it all begin?  
The specifics of where and when we first met remain vague.  
You might think it's just age catching up with me, clouding my memory,  
but even if you'd asked me a decade ago, I doubt I could have answered.

According to what I often call my "Amida Lottery Theory of Life,"  
I can trace the lines, but I’ve forgotten where they start.  
Oh well, it doesn’t really matter.  
This "Amida Lottery" indeed has a starting point,  
which is the moment and place of our birth.  
I understand that this is significant,  
but think of the countless turns we take after that.  
If I hadn’t attended that school, I wouldn’t have met that friend.  
Without meeting them, I wouldn’t have taken up that sport.  
I met that person because I decided to go out that day.  
Skipping school that one time led me to read that book.  
A series of seemingly trivial coincidences and choices  
can alter future events, bringing fortune or misfortune.

The other day, someone said to me, "If you hadn’t done that job,  
our children wouldn’t exist."  
That’s right; because two people met through that work,  
those kids came into the world. Amazing, isn’t it?  
My "Amida Lottery" intersects and overlaps with other people's lotteries.  
Come to think of it, even with those I haven’t met yet,  
I am connected through some form of expression.  
It’s intriguingly uncanny.  
My "Amida Lottery" seems poised to take many more turns, but that’s okay.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" today.  
Tracing back the twists and turns can be quite fascinating.
