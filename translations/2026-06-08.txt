A bit like the acknowledgments tucked behind the afterword of a book.  
Shigesato Itoi

・I keep writing about this every day, but it's only because our lives and our thoughts are completely wrapped up in the "Joy of Living Fair." Sorry about that.

From June 1st, it was a week of non-stop activity, and if you count the setup and teardown, it stretched to ten days. We made it through with "Safety first, fun second." It was a blast, don't you think? As usual, I can't help but praise my team—everyone was amazed at how much strength we had gained. "Did we really get this strong?" they'd say, referring to the crew and the vendors. Thanks to all the thinking, adapting, and hard work you put in, everything went more smoothly. Even during the teardown and the celebrations, smiles were blooming everywhere. Even when we were working at breakneck speed, there were no harsh commands or raised voices; everyone moved naturally, like a well-oiled machine. I truly respect everyone who worked here. You all looked so cool, and most importantly, you seemed to be having fun. Whenever I asked, "Did you have fun?" like a greeting, the immediate "I did!" always made me happy.

To all the vendors, thank you for joining us on this journey. And to the temporary staff, you were truly amazing. Whether you've been with us since our first event in Roppongi, joined as a family, traveled from afar, made friends here, are one of our partners at "Hobonichi," or perhaps even someone older than me, a former crew member, or a mother who finally joined us after waiting for her kids to grow up—thank you. Many have already reserved their spot for the next event, saying, "I want to do it again."

Immense gratitude to the families, friends, and neighbors who supported the crew working here. To Sumitomo Realty, our "landlords" for this venue, and to everyone involved in setup, logistics, security, and more—thank you! Akiko Kondo, Tobi, who was a daily presence, thank you! And to the "Hobonichi Techo" team who kept things running at headquarters in Kanda throughout this period, thank you. Lastly, thank you to all the visitors who participated!

Thank you for coming to "Hobonichi" today. As for me, I lay back on my sofa at home, and my legs cramped up. Yours truly, Itoi.
