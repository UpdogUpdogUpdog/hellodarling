However, I also know the answers aren't always reliable. Yesterday, my boss at home mentioned, "I put some soil in a flowerpot, and seeds about the size of soybeans were mixed in, and they've started sprouting." After comparing photos with a search engine, it seemed to be "this thing called tamarind." Isn't that nice? "Tamarind" has a pleasant ring to it. It almost makes me hear music from far-off India. But the next day, when the stem grew a bit, it seemed different. Without jumping to conclusions, it started to look like "edamame." That's when my "neighbor" ChatGPT comes into play. I took a photo of the plant, explained about the soybean-sized seeds mixed in the soil and their growth, and asked, "What is this?" Then (skipping some details), it said, "It's probably an avocado!" Think it over, confirm the questions before answering, please. "It's definitely a morning glory," it declared. Hey now, I cautioned it! Eventually, I guided it to the answer of "soybean or pea." When I asked why it made such mistakes, it gave a long, proper excuse, but... enough already...

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan" today. In the end, the first "tamarind" was the most enjoyable after all.
