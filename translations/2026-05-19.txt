Everyone seems to have this surplus of untapped potential.  
—Shigesato Itoi

・It seems like so many people are thinking, "If only someone would clearly tell me, 'Do this or that,' and give me specific instructions on what I should be doing, then I could really show what I'm capable of." Or perhaps they're thinking, "If only someone would present me with a goal or a bright future, I would give it my all, even if it meant exhausting myself completely." I suspect that might be the case. There are probably countless people who think, "The reason I can't fully unleash my abilities is that I'm not given a clear task or I haven't discovered what I truly want to do."

In dramas, there's often a respected leader, and the story revolves around everyone working together to "make him a man," so to speak. You see it in sports, battles, and corporate tales. The members of these stories' teams already "know what needs to be done," so no matter how tough, impossible, or sacrificial it might be, they just have to throw themselves into it with everything they've got. In those scenarios, it's easy to think, "If I were in that situation, I could give it my all," or blame it on the lack of a solid vision from those above. I get it; it feels like there's this surplus of energy just waiting to be used.

But, you know, situations where "all you need to do is give it your best once you know what needs to be done" are becoming rarer in this day and age, unlike in the past. If you truly "know what needs to be done," wouldn’t that be something for AI to handle instead? You might have a ladder and the strength to climb it, but if you don't know where to place it or what you're climbing toward, what's the point? In a time like this, having something you want or need to do is an incredible blessing. Maybe creating opportunities to use your potential is what creativity is all about.
