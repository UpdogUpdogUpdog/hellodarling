10,000 Times Over  
by Shigesato Itoi

・Seems like someone over at "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" noticed.  
I've been writing these short pieces for so long, and without realizing it, I might have hit the 10,000 mark.  
I mean, it's not like I was counting each one as I wrote.  
Every year when June 6th rolls around, I do think about how long I've been at this.

As for writing every day, I've touched on that theme many times here.  
Some people are amazed at my consistency, so it's an easy topic to write about.  
They ask if it's tough, or if there are days when I just don't want to write.

Originally, I started this whole thing because I wanted people to say things like that.  
When launching "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun," I thought, "What can I do that, if I just give it a shot, will make people happy?"  
And the answer was, "Write every day without fail."  
I wanted to be like a shopkeeper who shows up at the store every day.  
Whether my writing is good or bad, I have no idea.  
In cooking terms, it's like aiming for "as long as it's not terrible" (this is borrowed from a famous quote).  
There's no secret to keeping it up; I just do it because I decided to.  
It's something anyone can do if they try, but people say, "That's exactly what I can't manage."  
To keep it up every single day, you need to avoid getting seriously ill, think of ways to manage when things get tough, and make some small adjustments as part of living.  
Compared to actual effort, this feels pretty low-cost.

So, when I realized I might have passed the 10,000 mark, I got curious about when exactly that was and asked ChatGPT.  
Turns out, the 10,000th day was October 21, 2025.  
And apparently, today's piece is number 10,145.  
When you write 10,000 pieces, your foolishness, your flaws, and your carelessness all get exposed.  
That might have been the best thing about this whole endeavor.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" again today.  
Doing what you can do if you try is surprisingly doable, you know.
