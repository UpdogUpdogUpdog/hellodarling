An Old Man Placing a Giant Stone in the Middle of the Road  
Shigesato Itoi

In the twilight years of his life, he once said with a rather strong tone, "These days, everyone talks about communication this and communication that. I'm thinking of placing a huge stone right in the middle of all that communication to mess things up." He was so earnest that I took his words seriously, even though I didn't fully grasp them. Since "communication" is akin to "traffic," I imagined something like a road. There was old Yoshimoto, drenched in sweat, struggling to place an enormous stone in the middle of this "road" where information flows, blocking the way. It conjured up an anime-like scene in my mind. Just that image was amusing enough, but I couldn't quite understand why Yoshimoto wanted to do such a thing. It couldn't just be a matter of being contrary, but at the time, it was simply amusing.

Yet, the phrase left a lasting impression on me, and I couldn't forget the image of "placing a giant stone in the middle of communication." It was like having a poster permanently hung in my mind.

Recently, though I can't articulate it well, I feel like I'm starting to understand. When "communication" is overly prioritized, the focus shifts to efficiency in conveying messages, organizing for clarity, and methods to communicate more. Now, it truly feels like we've entered such an era. The trend of "verbalization" and the advancement of "AI" are part of it too. Words are being stripped of their excesses, their blemishes, their wrinkles. Just as faces and bodies undergo cosmetic surgery, words too are being nudged toward an "ideal" of easy understanding. It's in the midst of such heavy traffic that Yoshimoto places his giant stone. That giant stone could be something like "poetry," for instance.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan." My thoughts might not have been conveyed perfectly today either, but thank you for stopping by.
