Even now, the flowers are blooming.  
Shigesato Itoi

・They say "cherry blossoms," as if that's all there is.  
When people think of Japan, they immediately picture cherry blossoms,  
as if it's the only country where they bloom.  
Sure, cherry blossoms have that superstar aura about them,  
but hey, once the cherry blossoms have fallen,  
vivid azaleas bloom at knee height as you walk.  
There are so many of them everywhere that nobody bothers to go azalea-viewing.  
Even today, just like in the old days, elementary school kids  
pluck the azalea flowers and sip the nectar.  
And just like kids from the past, they exclaim, "Sweet!"  
Before long, roses begin to bloom in the hedges and shrubs.  
Roses are so beloved that they're always available at florists,  
so they don't really give the impression of starting to bloom somewhere.  
But this is just the right season for planted roses to bloom.  
Not just the grand flowers meant for sale,  
but even the modest blooms are charming in their own way.  
As the rainy season comes to mind, the plum trees bear fruit.  
Ah, that's right, "tsuyu" (rainy season) is written with the characters for "plum rain."  
The flowers had bloomed in the cold of February.  
Eventually, depending on the region, apricots start to ripen too.  
I love apricots.  
I'll probably make apricot jam myself again this year.  
Of all jams, apricot jam is my favorite.  
By the end of the rainy season, it's already summer, and various wild plants  
grow lush and lively.  
They may be a nuisance, but I envy their exuberance.  
Oh yes, morning glories start unfurling their vines and blooming here and there.  
Even in cities or the countryside, tall sunflowers bloom.  
Being simply huge is a great trait, sunflowers.

They say "cherry blossoms," but the floral calendar continues beyond them.  
Each season, even if they're not stars, good actors bloom—  
whether we notice them or not.  
Just like there's always music playing somewhere, even now, the flowers are blooming.

Thank you for visiting "Almost Daily" today.  
My gratitude goes to those who tend to the town's gardens.
