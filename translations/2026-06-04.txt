Sailing on the "Hobo Nikkan" Ship  
By Shigesato Itoi

Once upon a time—perhaps about 28 years ago—I realized that the way we work could be likened to a "ship." Back then, organizations (or companies) were often seen as hierarchical structures, like a tree or a tournament bracket, with an important peak at the top of a triangle. But if you were to knock over that triangle and make it flat, it wouldn't be about top and bottom anymore; it would become about front and back. The entire structure would start to resemble a "ship" moving forward. That's the image that came to mind.

On a ship, everyone on board is crucial, aren't they? Deciding which direction to go, reading the weather, keeping the engines running, loading and unloading cargo, preparing meals—even peeling potatoes—all are vital tasks. Everyone must protect each other to make it work. There's an old saying about ships: "Below the planks, it's hell," because if it sinks, you're nothing but sea foam. I'm not trying to emphasize the life-or-death aspect, but I thought the idea of everyone working together on the same ship was a good one, even though we're on land.

That's when I started using the term "crew members." It's not a very conventional term, so I use it carefully, but in the right context, I still refer to us as "crew." After all, it's just another way to say "crew," isn't it? Typically, the crew members handle their specialized departments, but when something comes up, they do tasks outside their usual roles. They help each other as individuals because that's how it is on a ship. You can't get by without it, and helping one another is the basis for being part of the crew. I think that feeling is shared among those who recently joined "Hobo Nikkan" and those who have been here for a long time. If you're part of this ship, even if you're a part-timer, it probably feels the same.

I have a feeling that, perhaps, even our customers (passengers) are on the same ship with us.

Thank you for coming to "Hobo Nikkan" today. Yesterday, our ship weathered a typhoon. We're sailing happily.
