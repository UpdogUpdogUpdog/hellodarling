Today's Bits and Pieces  
Shigesato Itoi

・So, it's officially called a "Scorching Day" now.  
That refers to any day when the temperature hits 40 degrees Celsius or more.  
It's fascinating to witness the moment when something "social" or "official" gets its name.  
From now on, throughout this country's history,  
any day over 40 degrees will be formally known as a "Scorching Day."  
Even if some people call it an "Inferno Day" or an "Extreme Heat Day,"  
those are just nicknames.  
They'll be corrected with, "Officially, it's a Scorching Day."  
Whether you call them "sneakers," "sports shoes," or "footgear,"  
you're free to choose,  
but a day over 40 degrees Celsius is only accurately a "Scorching Day."

・On a different note,  
I wonder if the thing we think about most is, "What am I really thinking?"  
It's not the same as "finding oneself."  
It's more like genuinely not knowing what our thoughts are.  
That's why, when I come across a hint or a clue about it, I'm thrilled.  
Or if I figure out why I came to think that way,  
understanding the cause or reason, it makes me quite happy.  
If someone never questions, "What am I thinking?"  
I feel like that person might be a bit of a puzzle.

・Anyway,  
I made up my mind to eat natto every day,  
and I gave it a fair shot, but eventually, I stopped.  
It's not that I've grown to dislike natto; it tastes good when I eat it.  
But I got tired of it. I couldn't love it enough to want it daily.  
Just knowing it's "very good for you" wasn't a strong enough reason to keep it up every day.  
These experiences of "I tried, but it didn't work out" accumulate quite a bit over time.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nikkan Itoi Shinbun" today.  
Weighing myself in the morning and at night is something I've actually managed to keep up with.
