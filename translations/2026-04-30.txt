Chatty Omelettes  
by Shigesato Itoi

・I might actually be a bit of a proverb enthusiast.  
  Proverbs often come to mind, and I frequently savor their meanings.  
  There are proverbs that completely contradict each other, but that doesn't make them a paradox.  
  "Slow and steady wins the race," and "Strike while the iron is hot"—both exist.  
  When you're rushing too much, it's wise to remember "Slow and steady wins the race."  
  When you're dithering and lack the drive to act, "Strike while the iron is hot" is the way to go.  
  It's all about using them as a guide for judgment and confidence in the moment.  
  It's a matter of "time and place," just like "a fool with a tool."  
  Bringing daikon radish from oden on a field trip is a mismatch, but so is eating a pork bun at a funeral.  
  Ah, I'm saying something that doesn't serve as a metaphor at all.  
  Just let it go in one ear and out the other.

My favorite proverb at the moment is, "Even a round egg can be cut into a square."  
  To be honest, the expression "round egg" is a bit problematic.  
  Are eggs generally considered "round"?  
  Isn't it more accurate to say that eggs are "egg-shaped"?  
  Either way, if you cut it into a square, a square it becomes.  
  This concept can be applied to just about anything.

Let’s whimsically slice an egg into a square.  
  A rainy day is perfect weather for trying out a new umbrella.  
  Inari sushi is categorized as sushi, but isn't it, in a way, a "tofu dish"?  
  After all, it's thinly sliced tofu, fried and simmered in sweet-savory sauce, eaten with vinegared rice.  
  Becoming the best in the world means,  
  "Wait, I can't become the best anymore?"  
  because it turns into "I am the best in the world."  
  If you think about it this way, that's perfectly fine.  
  Just turning an egg into various other things is enough.  
  Sunny-side-up, omelettes, scrambled eggs, chawanmushi,  
  omelets, boiled eggs, onsen tamago—all made from the same egg.  
  Transforming them into different dishes brings joy to more people.  
  I'm grateful for being able to write about this today.

Thank you for visiting "Hobo Nichi" again today.  
Lately, I've been a bit pressed for time, so I'm taking it easy.
