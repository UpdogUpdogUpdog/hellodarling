Reflecting on "A World Without Me"
by Shigesato Itoi

It was quite a while ago, but there was a time when I glimpsed a world without myself in it. Of course, it was just an image in my mind.

One night, after finishing work as usual, I was tired and sleepy, ready to call it a day. I walked into the bedroom, probably leaving the lights off, and caught sight of the bed where I was about to sleep. My wife was already fast asleep in the bed next to mine, and the bed I was about to occupy was empty. If I didn't lie down there, it would remain unoccupied. It was nothing special, just the way things are. Yet, in that moment, a thought struck me: "Ah, this is a world without me." I could suddenly visualize a world that continued on without me in it. It felt as though I was a ghost observing this "world without me," and the empty bed before me was no different from that vision. A wave of something welled up inside me, a profound sadness. I can't quite remember if I cried, but it was a sorrow unlike any I had known before, and that feeling stayed with me. I've even shared the story with close friends, sometimes with a touch of humor. I'd say, "Come to think of it, the person sleeping soundly there would be my widow," with a bit of discontent. But truly, in that moment, I was overwhelmed by sadness. The thought of saying goodbye to everyone and everything, and everything existing without me, was heart-wrenching.

Since then, I haven't had such thoughts before bed, and now it's become a story I can recount with a hint of nostalgia. At least for now, I don't feel the same deep sadness. It's more akin to loneliness, perhaps. I'm not entirely sure. When I think of those who have already said goodbye to this world, I sometimes recall that "world without me" feeling.
